# Market segment research

Build a factual snapshot of a market segment for later SEO, content, competitor,
or positioning work. Keep observation separate from any later recommendation.

## Define the research boundary

Record the segment, geography, observation date, query or competitor scope,
result depth, and any known tool, location, account, or personalisation limits.
Keep the project's own pages out of competitor samples unless the task
explicitly requests an audit of them.

## Research the visible landscape

For search and query research, record exact queries, returned result types,
visible categories, labels, modifiers, directories, recurring page types,
counted observations, and sampled absences. State which search interface or
tool produced the sample and what it did not expose.

For competitor and provider research, record the sampled pages, provider roles,
service categories, visible claims, terminology, page structures, offers,
geographic scope, and repeated or contrasting features. Provider pages establish
what providers say and how they present the market; they do not demonstrate
competence, quality, effectiveness, or audience preference.

For directories and taxonomies, record how the source groups, labels, filters,
and displays the segment. A directory category establishes that directory's
classification, not a universal market definition.

## Record observations

Use stable `S##` IDs. For each material observation, retain the exact query or
source, geography, date, sampling method or result depth, and what was visibly
observed. Count repeated features when useful and keep different samples
separate when their methods or scopes differ.

Report observations directly. Do not invent search volume, demand, ranking
stability, audience need, or market size. A result that did not appear in one
sample is a sampled absence, not proof of absence or an automatic opportunity.

## Hand off the research

State what was checked, the strongest observed patterns and differences, the
limits of the sample, and what remains unknown. Leave SEO targeting, content
opportunities, positioning, design, and implementation decisions to the later
task unless the user explicitly requests that separate work.
