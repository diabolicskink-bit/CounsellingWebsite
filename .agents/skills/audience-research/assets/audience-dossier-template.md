## Research coverage

formal sources, number and type of public sources, and any explicit exclusions.

## Key findings

Summarise the strongest cross-source findings in concise, evidence-backed
points. Use as many findings as the evidence warrants, without a fixed minimum
or maximum. Cite the source IDs and include headline percentages, counts,
ranges, sample sizes, source-set recurrence, comparisons, or reported
qualitative categories where they make the findings concrete. Preserve material
subgroup and setting distinctions. Do not turn the section into implications,
recommendations, communication priorities, or unsupported abstractions.

## Detailed findings

Organise the fuller evidence by research question or subject. Retain the study
context, variation, contradictions, and limitations that the key findings
necessarily compress. Intentional repetition of the strongest figures is
acceptable; do not repeat routine method detail already available in the source
register.

### Reported negative experiences

Synthesize the reported patterns, then include the actual categories, counts,
percentages, or qualitative themes from key studies.

### Reported positive experiences
