# Website Design Quality Review

Use this checklist before delivering a visual critique or implemented website change. Apply checks proportionally to scope; do not claim a check was completed unless it was actually performed.

## Contents

1. Task and content
2. Thumbnail distance
3. Reading distance
4. Interaction distance
5. Detail distance
6. Responsive and accessibility
7. Performance and resilience
8. Distinctiveness
9. Delivery

## 1. Task and content

- Does the page make its purpose clear?
- Can the audience locate the answer or next step?
- Does section order reflect visitor questions rather than internal departments?
- Are important claims supported near the point of decision?
- Are costs, constraints, eligibility, privacy, and consequences candid?
- Is representative content used rather than convenient placeholder lengths?
- Are fabricated claims, clients, awards, metrics, and testimonials absent?

## 2. Thumbnail distance

- Is there a clear focal point?
- Are dominant, supporting, and quiet levels visible?
- Is visual weight intentionally balanced?
- Does the page silhouette show meaningful rhythm?
- Do section changes correspond to changes in meaning?
- Can the primary action be located without reading everything?
- Is there enough calm for emphasis to work?

## 3. Reading distance

- Are page title, section heading, summary, body, metadata, evidence, and action roles distinct?
- Is prose measure comfortable for the typeface and language?
- Are line height and paragraph spacing appropriate to measure and density?
- Do line breaks support meaning without becoming brittle?
- Does proximity accurately group labels, fields, captions, claims, and evidence?
- Are headings descriptive and scan-friendly?
- Are colour and type contrast concentrated on meaningful information?
- Is body text substantial enough in contrast, size, and weight?

## 4. Interaction distance

- Do interactive elements look interactive?
- Are links distinguishable from plain text?
- Are hover, focus, active, visited, disabled, loading, empty, success, and error states present where relevant?
- Is focus visible and unobscured?
- Is keyboard order logical?
- Are controls labelled and instructions available before errors occur?
- Does validation identify the problem and recovery path?
- Does motion explain state, continuity, relationship, or sequence?
- Is repeated or automatic motion non-blocking?
- Is direct feedback immediate?

## 5. Detail distance

- Are icons optically aligned with text and consistent in style?
- Are borders, rules, shadows, radii, and texture part of one material logic?
- Are image crops intentional at each key viewport?
- Are captions visibly attached to their media?
- Are awkward widows, orphans, and heading wraps resolved where practical?
- Do real long and short values fit controls and components?
- Are one-off values intentional rather than accidental drift?
- Does polish come from resolving decisions rather than adding effects?

## 6. Responsive and accessibility

Inspect narrow, intermediate, and wide widths.

- Does content priority survive recomposition?
- Do related elements remain meaningfully adjacent?
- Is there horizontal overflow at ordinary zoom or enlarged text?
- Do images use appropriate crops and sources?
- Are tap targets large and separated enough?
- Does navigation remain understandable and operable?
- Does the design work with keyboard only?
- Do headings and landmarks preserve semantic structure?
- Do meaningful images have useful alternatives?
- Is colour never the only cue?
- Do text, controls, and focus indicators meet required contrast?
- Does content reflow at zoom without loss or overlap?
- Does reduced motion remove or replace problematic spatial movement?
- Do language direction and localisation remain viable where relevant?

## 7. Performance and resilience

- Are image dimensions reserved to prevent shift?
- Are responsive images sized appropriately?
- Are below-fold media and non-critical code deferred where sensible?
- Are fonts limited, subset, and loaded with compatible fallbacks?
- Do decorative effects create unnecessary paint, compositing, or script cost?
- Are animations based on performant properties where possible?
- Does the page remain understandable when fonts, images, or JavaScript are delayed?
- Are loading, offline, empty, and failure states handled when needed?
- Is console output free of relevant errors and warnings?

## 8. Distinctiveness

- Can every major choice be explained without “modern,” “clean,” “premium,” or “engaging”?
- Is there a governing idea recognisable beyond the hero asset?
- Do type, colour, image, material, composition, and motion describe the same character?
- Does layout respond to actual content?
- Is one signature behaviour or motif developed thoroughly?
- Are imperfections real consequences of material or process rather than filters?
- Would the design survive a transferability test against competitors?
- Does it avoid both trend mimicry and novelty that harms ordinary interaction?

## 9. Delivery

- Does the result satisfy the requested scope?
- Were unrelated user changes preserved?
- Were appropriate builds, tests, and visual checks run?
- Are remaining limitations stated plainly?
- Does the handoff lead with outcome, changed files, and verification rather than tool narration?
