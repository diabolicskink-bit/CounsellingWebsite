# Kink-client search and Australian provider-market evidence packet

- **Status:** Working evidence packet for integration
- **Audience boundary:** Adults for whom kink or BDSM is central, relevant, or
  incidental to counselling; provider pages are evidence of market wording and
  structure only
- **Geography:** Perth/Western Australia and Australia-wide online services
- **Observed:** 2026-07-16
- **Method:** Public web search in the research tool's unlogged, location- and
  personalisation-unknown context; exact-query samples plus opened current
  directory and provider pages

This packet contains search and market observations. It does not establish
search volume, stable rankings, provider competence, service quality, audience
preference, or any copy, SEO, content, or positioning recommendation. Vive's
own pages and listings were excluded from the provider sample and were not
audited.

## Questions examined

- What kinds of results appear for local, Australia-wide online, BDSM, and D/s
  counselling searches?
- Which provider roles and service categories appear beside kink/BDSM wording?
- Which kink-related labels and adjacent audience terms appear on a defined
  Australian provider-page sample?
- How do two prominent directories classify or display relevant providers?
- Which observations are absences or artefacts of this one search sample?

## Search method and limits

Four exact queries were run separately and the first 10 visible organic results
were coded. Sponsored placements, map packs, and private/personalised results
were not available in the research tool. A result was counted as relevant when
its visible title or extract named kink, BDSM, D/s, sex-positive/kink-allied
care, or a directly relevant provider/directory category. Duplicate URLs and
multiple pages from the same provider were retained in the raw result count but
identified as duplicates.

The result engine did not expose search volume, the searcher's precise
geolocation, a stable rank export, or every result feature. The observations
therefore describe only the returned sample on 2026-07-16.

### Cross-query observations

- The local query was dominated by Psychology Today and KAP surfaces rather
  than ten independent local practice sites. [S01]
- Adding `Australia online` returned interstate providers and national
  telehealth offers alongside location-category pages. [S02]
- `BDSM counselling Australia` returned several distinct service categories,
  including counselling and psychotherapy, sexology/sex therapy, education,
  coaching, workshops, and lessons. [S03]
- The narrower D/s query produced a mixed set in which only 2 of the first 10
  visible results explicitly named D/s or kink. This is a sampled result
  composition, not evidence of demand, ranking stability, or a market gap.
  [S04]

## Directory observations

### Kink Aware Professionals

On 2026-07-16, KAP's regional selector displayed 24 Australian listings. The
state counts displayed were New South Wales 3, Queensland 3, South Australia 1,
Victoria 9, and Western Australia 3; these sum to 19, leaving five Australian
records without one of those displayed state assignments. The WA page displayed
three listings, all under `Licensed Mental Health Professionals`. [S05]

KAP exposes separate `Poly Awareness Level`, `Kink Awareness Level`, and
`Swinger (Lifestyle) Awareness Level` fields. Values visible in the reviewed
Australian pages included `Friendly` and `Knowledgeable`; the WA page showed
`Knowledgeable` for the records whose fields rendered. It also displays
business category, education/training/certifications, remote versus physical
delivery, insurance, tags, and location. These are directory fields and
self-submitted claims, not independently assessed competence. [S05]

### Psychology Today Australia

Psychology Today's Perth category used the heading `Sex-Positive, Kink Allied
Psychologists and Therapists in Perth, WA` and the filter label `Sex-Positive &
Kink-Friendly`. The page exposed eight pagination links on the checked date.
The first visible page mixed Perth/WA premises, `Online Only`, and `Serves Area`
profiles, including interstate providers. [S06]

The page's own explanatory material stated that there are limited training
programs and unofficial accreditations rather than a generally required special
training pathway. That is Psychology Today's market explanation, not an
independent assessment of any listed practitioner. [S06]

Other counted observations:

- Only 2 of 18 sampled pages made kink the dedicated page or practitioner-brand
  subject: Progressive Therapeutic Collective's dedicated `Kink & BDSM` page
  and `The Kink Counsellor`. On the other 16, kink/BDSM appeared within a wider

  participants, using wording such as `polycules`, `throuples or more`, `2 or
  more people`, or `three or more people`. [S07, S08, S10, S12, S15, S24]
- Four sampled pages named a psychologist role; many others named counsellor,
  psychotherapist, sexologist, sex therapist, educator, coach, or more than one
  category. The roles and service labels overlapped rather than forming one
  uniform market category. [S07-S24]
- At least 10 pages visibly named a degree, professional registration, or
  professional membership on the sampled page. This count records disclosure
  of a credential signal, not whether it is relevant to kink work or evidence
  of competence. [S07, S11, S13, S15, S17-S23]
- Every sampled page paired kink/BDSM with at least one broader or adjacent
  context, such as sex/relationship work, ENM/polyamory, LGBTQIA+/gender,
  neurodivergence, sex work, disability, trauma, general mental health, or other
  marginalised experiences. [S07-S24]

## Contradictions and limits

- Directory taxonomies differ. KAP separates `Friendly` and `Knowledgeable`
  awareness levels, while Psychology Today combines `Sex-Positive` with
  `Kink-Friendly`/`Kink Allied`. The sampled independent pages added `aware`,
  `positive`, `informed`, and unqualified subject labels. [S05-S24]
- A location result did not consistently indicate a locally based practitioner.
  Psychology Today included `Online Only` and `Serves Area` profiles, and the
  Australia-wide query intentionally returned interstate providers. [S01,
  S02, S06]
- The service set contains regulated and self-regulated roles, education and
  coaching, individual and relationship work, sex therapy, general mental
  health care, and kink-specific pages. Search appearance alone does not resolve
  those differences. [S02-S04, S07-S24]
- Provider pages frequently co-located multiple audience labels. This establishes
  page wording and market bundling only; it does not show that the audiences,
  experiences, or service needs are interchangeable. [S07-S24]
- No Search Console, paid keyword tool, local rank tracker, incognito browser
  export, map-pack capture, or search-volume source was available. No claim is
  made about demand, ranking stability, or exhaustive market coverage.

## Remaining market gaps

- No exact search-volume or trend data were available.
- The sampled result engine did not provide a reproducible Perth IP location,
  sponsored-result capture, or map-pack export.
- Provider fees, availability, qualifications, and delivery arrangements are
  volatile and were recorded only where they contributed to the defined market
  questions; they were not audited for correctness.
- The provider sample is purposive and search-returned, not an exhaustive census
  of Australian kink-aware care.
