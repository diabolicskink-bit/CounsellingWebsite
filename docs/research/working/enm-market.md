# ENM, polyamory and non-monogamy search and provider-market packet

- **Status:** Working search-and-market evidence packet
- **Audience boundary:** Adults looking for counselling, psychotherapy,
  psychology, sex or relationship therapy, or adjacent professional support in
  which ethical/consensual non-monogamy, polyamory, open relationships, or
  relationship anarchy may be relevant
- **Geography:** Perth and Western Australia, plus online services presented as
  available in Australia
- **Observation date:** 2026-07-16
- **Evidence stream:** Search and market evidence only (`S##`)
- **Excluded:** Audience preference, provider competence or quality, demand,
  search volume, stable rankings, opportunities, recommendations, public-web
  discussion, and formal clinical evidence

Provider and directory pages in this packet establish only what providers and
directories visibly called, grouped, and offered on the observation date.

## Research coverage and method

Nine exact queries were run separately through the public web-search interface
available to the researcher. For each query that returned results, the first 10
standard webpage results shown by the interface were recorded in displayed
order. Reddit, news, academic, and other results shown after those first 10
standard webpage results were outside the counted sample. One query returned no
results in the interface. The resulting sample contains **80 result
appearances** across eight returning queries and **52 unique canonical webpage
URLs** after obvious duplicate URLs and redirects were normalised. [S01-S09]

The interface did not expose or allow control of IP-derived location, search
account, cookies, prior search history, ads, safe-search state, or
personalisation. No location parameter was available; geography was supplied
only in the query text. The interface returned indexed webpages and snippets,
not a complete Google or Bing results page, local map pack, ad set, result-count
estimate, or search-volume measure. The sample therefore records this
interface's dated output, not a neutral or stable Perth search ranking.

## Key findings

1. The exact acronym query behaved differently from longer descriptive queries:
   `ENM therapist Perth` returned no results, while the Perth queries containing
   `ethical non-monogamy` or `polyamory` returned multiple local provider and
   directory pages. [S01-S03]

2. `WA` was geographically ambiguous in this interface. Six of the first 10
   results for `non-monogamy therapist WA` visibly concerned Washington State,
   while one visibly concerned Perth, Western Australia. [S04]

3. In the coded **19-page Australian provider/service sample**, the primary
   visible service category was counselling, psychotherapy, or relationship
   therapy on 11 pages; psychology on two; sexology or sex therapy on five; and
   coaching/education expressly described as not psychotherapy on one. Several
   pages used more than one professional or service label, so the count assigns
   one dominant category to each page only for description. [S16-S34]

4. Within those 19 pages, `poly`, `polyamory`, or `polyamorous` appeared on 16;
   `ethical non-monogamy`, `ethically non-monogamous`, or `ENM` on 13;
   `consensual non-monogamy`, `consensually non-monogamous`, or `CNM` on four;
   `open relationship(s)` on six; `relationship anarchy` on five; and
   `swinging` on five. Counts overlap. [S16-S34]

5. Eighteen of the 19 reviewed provider pages or their returned search snippets
   mentioned online, video, telephone, or telehealth delivery; 12 explicitly
   described Australia-wide or across-Australia availability. Eight were
   Perth/WA-linked in their own page or a reviewed directory record. These
   groups overlap. [S12, S16-S34]

6. ENM wording was commonly placed alongside other audience or practice labels:
   16 of 19 pages also visibly named LGBTQIA+/queer communities, 12 named
   neurodivergence or neuro-affirming practice, 11 named kink/BDSM, and six
   named sex workers or sex-work affirmation. These are page co-occurrences,
   not evidence that the audiences or service needs are interchangeable.
   [S16-S34]

7. Eight of 19 pages explicitly described multi-person configurations using
   wording such as `polycules`, `throuples`, `triads`, `relationship groups`,
   `constellations`, `wider relationship network`, or `relationships (3+)`.
   One Perth couples page welcomed polyamorous and ENM relationships while also
   stating that sessions were limited to two people because of room size.
   [S18, S19, S22, S23, S25-S27, S29, S34]

8. Directory taxonomies were not equivalent. Psychology Today used the category
   `Open Relationships Non-Monogamy`; Come Back to Bed used
   `Non-Monogamy Allied Clinician Directory`; KAP separately recorded `Poly
   Awareness Level`, `Kink Awareness Level`, and `Swinger (Lifestyle) Awareness
   Level`; Poly Friendly Professionals separated licensed/registered
   psychotherapists from non-licensed therapists and other professions.
   [S10-S13]

9. Directory labels and visible profile detail did not always align. Psychology
   Today's filtered Perth page displayed `Show 168 Counsellors`, but only two of
   the 20 first-page excerpts visibly mentioned consensual/non-monogamy. Come
   Back to Bed's ENM directory contained 43 clinician entries, including four
   WA-linked entries, while none of those four visible `Relevant areas of
   interest` fields named ENM/polyamory. Both observations concern excerpt or
   field visibility; they do not negate the directories' category or the
   providers' self-selection. [S10, S11]

### Directory and taxonomy observations

#### Psychology Today

The opened Perth page used the breadcrumb/category `Open Relationships
Non-Monogamy` and the heading `Open Relationships Non-Monogamy Counselling in
Perth, WA`. Search-result titles for related routes used `Find Polyamory & ENM
Therapists in Perth, WA`. The page exposed `In Person` and `Online` controls,
professional listings, issues, therapy types, age, payment, sexuality,
language, faith, price, and sliding-scale filters. [S10]

The filtered page displayed `Show 168 Counsellors`, nine pagination links, and
20 profile cards on the first page. Two of those 20 visible card excerpts named
`consensual non-monogamy` or `non-monogamy`; the other 18 did not use ENM/poly
wording in their captured excerpts. The page FAQ also stated that a person
could find success with a therapist who did not specialise in non-monogamous
relationships if a trusting bond formed. These are directory wording and
excerpt observations, not outcome evidence. [S10]

#### Come Back to Bed

The `Non-Monogamy Allied Clinician Directory` stated that it collected
clinicians who self-identified as open to or passionate about working with
non-monogamous relationships and expressly said the entries were not
professional recommendations. Its fields included role, practice, `Relevant
areas of interest`, online sessions, and Medicare/private-health status. [S11]

The single rendered page contained **43 clinician entries**: ACT 1, NSW 16,
Queensland 5, South Australia 1, Tasmania 1, Western Australia 3, Victoria 9,
and `Online Only` 7. One online-only entry was also labelled as based in WA, for
four WA-linked entries in total. None of the four WA-linked entries' visible
interest fields specifically said ENM/polyamory; one had no visible interest
field. The directory-wide self-identification statement still applied. [S11]

#### Kink Aware Professionals (KAP)

KAP's Australia filter displayed **24 Australia listings** and state-filter
counts of NSW 3, Queensland 3, South Australia 1, Victoria 9, and Western
Australia 3; the displayed state counts summed to 19, leaving five Australia
listings without one of those visible state classifications. The directory
used `Business Category`, business tags, internet-based-business and insurance
fields, plus separate `Poly Awareness Level`, `Kink Awareness Level`, and
`Swinger (Lifestyle) Awareness Level` fields. Visible level values included
`Aware`, `Friendly`, and `Knowledgeable`. Categories included `Licensed Mental
Health Professionals`, `Health and Wellness (non M.D.)`, and `Other
Professionals`. [S12]

#### Polyamory-Friendly Professionals and Society of Australian Sexologists

The global `Polyamory-Friendly Professionals Directory` described itself as a
listing of professionals sensitive to polyamorous clientele. Its visible
taxonomy counted 2,360 `Psychotherapists - Licensed or Registered`, 305
`Non-licensed Therapists`, and smaller legal, medical, bodywork, family,
spiritual, and other professional categories. It expressly said listing did
not indicate personal screening or endorsement. The rendered landing page did
not expose an Australia count or filter to this method. [S13]

The Society of Australian Sexologists member directory used `State`,
`Registration`, and `Accreditation` filters, including Western Australia. Its
visible directory description distinguished psychosexual therapists from
sexuality educators and advised checking specific issue competence. No
ENM/polyamory filter was visible in the rendered text. [S14]

### Positive, neutral, and contradictory observations

- The exact acronym-only Perth query returned no results, while longer Perth
  queries returned specific local pages and directories. This was inconsistent
  query behaviour within the same interface. [S01-S03]
- `WA` retrieved both Washington State and Western Australia. The result set
  could not be treated as locally resolved without reading titles, snippets,
  and URLs. [S04]
- Several ENM-specific pages also explicitly welcomed monogamous relationships
  or framed ENM as one of several relationship orientations. [S27-S29, S33]
- Multi-person work was expressly offered on eight pages, while one Perth page
  welcomed ENM/polyamorous structures but limited sessions to two people for a
  physical-space reason. [S18, S19, S22, S23, S25-S27, S29, S34]
- National online access was common in the page sample, but it was not uniform:
  PolyPsychology expressly excluded Victoria, some local pages stated only
  online/in-person without a national service area, and one captured page did
  not state a delivery mode. [S19, S22, S23]
- A coaching service appeared repeatedly for therapy/counselling queries and
  used therapist wording in its returned search title, while its page expressly
  stated that it did not provide psychotherapy or therapy. [S05, S07-S09, S34]
- Psychology Today's category count and Come Back to Bed's directory-wide
  self-identification were broader than the ENM wording visible in many profile
  excerpts or interest fields. This may reflect hidden profile metadata,
  truncated excerpts, or directory inclusion criteria that are not repeated in
  each visible field; the method could not distinguish among these explanations.
  [S10, S11]
- The Victorian Pride Centre listing combined individual, couples, and
  relationship-group counselling with a monthly peer-support group, showing
  counselling and peer support as separate named services on one listing. [S15]

### Sampled absences and technical limits

- No search volume, trend, click, conversion, or demand data were available.
- Search-result positions were observed once and were not stability-tested.
- The interface supplied no ad, local-pack, knowledge-panel, or related-query
  view, and no estimated total result count.
- The zero-result acronym query was not repeated through a logged-in browser or
  another search engine, so it remains an interface-specific observation.
- The first 10 results were sampled; deeper result pages were not counted.
- Duplicate appearances from the same provider, directory, or domain were
  retained in query-level composition counts because they occupied separate
  returned result positions; provider-page synthesis deduplicated them.
- Search snippets may be stale, truncated, or generated from text not prominent
  on the live page. Page observations were checked against the rendered text
  available to the researcher where possible.
- Provider self-descriptions, qualifications, registration, lived experience,
  availability, fees, testimonials, and service claims were not independently
  verified.
- A directory's inclusion, awareness label, or filter category did not establish
  provider competence, suitability, endorsement, or clinical quality.
- Pages absent from the sample may exist. A missing label in a captured excerpt
  or field is not proof that the provider does not use it elsewhere.
