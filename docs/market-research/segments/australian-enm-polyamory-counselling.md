# Australian ENM and polyamory counselling market-research dossier

- **Owner context:** Vive Counselling, operated by Joel in Perth, offering
  individual counselling in person in Perth and online across Australia.
- **Segment boundary:** Australian adult counselling, psychology,
  psychotherapy, sexology and related relationship services that publicly name
  ethical or consensual non-monogamy, polyamory, open relationships,
  relationship anarchy or a close equivalent.
- **Geographic decision layers:** Perth/Western Australia as the local target;
  Australia as the serviceable online market; other Australian cities as
  comparators; selected United States, United Kingdom, Canadian and New Zealand
  sources as learning markets only.
- **Observed:** 17 July 2026.
- **Operation:** New dossier.
- **Status:** Canonical decision-support research. It includes owner-specific
  interpretation, recommendations and stated assumptions. It is not approved
  public copy, audience research, a provider recommendation, a competence
  assessment or permission to implement a site change.

## Executive synthesis

ENM and polyamory counselling are visibly established service concepts in
Australia, but this research did not obtain a defensible measure of segment
demand. Eight fixed-depth query samples returned direct providers and
specialist directories, while a purposive sample found 16 direct provider
units. That establishes discoverability and supply, not query volume, viable
lead volume or revenue. The responsible commercial reading is **a credible,
bounded opportunity worth testing**, not a proven large market. [Q01-Q08;
C01-C16; D01]

The strongest practical opportunity for Vive is one clear, indexable service
page for **individual counselling for people in ethical non-monogamous,
polyamorous and open relationships**. It should serve two related geographic
jobs: establish Joel as a Perth option and make the Australia-wide online offer
unmistakable. The national reach is strategically important, not a decorative
SEO phrase: Joel's local community familiarity can build trust, but known-person
and dual-relationship boundaries will make some Perth prospects unsuitable.
The wider serviceable market provides reach without asking Joel to blur those
boundaries. [C03-C04; C08; C11; C13; C15-C16; owner/project context]

The defensible position is not the generic claim `ENM affirming`. Many providers
already use inclusion or specialist language. Vive's more distinctive and
truthful combination is:

1. individual rather than ongoing couple or polycule counselling;
2. psychodynamic and attachment-informed depth;
3. ENM can be the subject of counselling, relevant context, an area being
   explored, or unrelated background;
4. a non-steering stance that does not treat either monogamy or non-monogamy as
   the required outcome; and
5. lived familiarity supported by an explicit professional role, scope and
   limits.

The existing draft route already creates most of the asset required, but it is
currently outside production discovery: it is noindex/nofollow, absent from
production navigation and the sitemap, and its final individual-service
boundary still needs to be made exact. The priority is therefore to finish one
page, index it, connect it to the site and measure it. The evidence does not
justify duplicate synonym pages, Perth suburb pages, a large glossary, paid
search or a general ENM content library yet. [Q01-Q08; C01-C16; C19-C26;
project current scope]

## Decision question and intended use

### Primary decision question

Should Vive make Australian ENM/polyamory counselling an actively discoverable
service segment, and if so, what proposition, geographic reach, discovery
channels and validation plan are justified by current evidence?

### Research questions

1. Which ENM/polyamory query formulations visibly surface providers and
   intermediaries in Perth and Australia?
2. What direct, adjacent and intermediary supply is visible, and how do roles,
   configuration, delivery, price and enquiry paths differ?
3. What evidence demonstrates actual demand, and where is demand unmeasured?
4. Which overseas page, terminology and trust patterns may transfer to an
   Australian service, and which are jurisdiction-specific?
5. What do the combined findings mean for Vive's offer, page, channels,
   measurement and next decisions?

## Owner and service context used in the synthesis

The recommendations are grounded in these current project facts rather than in
a hypothetical generic practice:

- Vive provides counselling for adults from Perth and online across Australia.
- Joel's current core service is individual counselling; the ENM page must not
  imply ongoing couples, relationship-group or polycule therapy.
- The counselling approach is psychodynamic and attachment informed.
- The current public fee is A$120 for 50 minutes, with a free 15-minute
  consultation and no referral required.
- The intended stance is inclusive and non-pathologising without making the
  client's relationship structure the presumed problem.
- ENM may be the presenting subject, meaningful context, something a person is
  exploring, or unrelated to why they seek counselling.
- Local community familiarity has two effects: it can support credibility, but
  known-person and overlapping-community boundaries can make some prospective
  clients unsuitable.
- The existing ENM/polyamory route is a substantial draft but remains
  noindex/nofollow and outside the production navigation and sitemap.
- There is no first-party segment dataset available in this task from Search
  Console, analytics conversions, enquiries, bookings or revenue.

If any of those facts changes, the recommendations should be revisited.

professional regulation, insurance, directory rules, market scale or actual
service capability. A useful overseas pattern is localised and validated; it is
not copied because the source market appears more mature.

## Method and evidence quality

### Query sample

On 17 July 2026, eight exact written queries were run through a general web
search interface. The first ten numeric general-web entries for each query were
retained and classified, giving 80 page-level observations. Multiple pages or
directory routes from one domain were retained because the unit at this stage
was the returned page. Direct provider identities were then deduplicated.

The interface did not expose the underlying engine, actual search location,
account state, personalisation, device, SafeSearch, ads, maps/local pack,
autocomplete, People Also Ask or dependable total-result counts. The results
are therefore a reproducible written-query sample for this interface and date,
not a Google ranking report and not a substitute for searching from Perth.

### Provider and intermediary sample

The purposive direct-provider sample contains 16 public provider/practice
units: seven in Western Australia and nine national or other-state comparators.
Inclusion required explicit first-party or provider-authored ENM, polyamory,
non-monogamy, open-relationship or relationship-anarchy language connected to
an eligible counselling, psychology, psychotherapy, sexology or relationship
service. Two overlapping coaching or peer/community offers were kept adjacent.

Sampling preserved differences in professional role, geography, delivery,
individual versus relationship configuration, specialist versus inclusion-page
presentation, public pricing and enquiry route. It stopped once focused query
and directory follow-ups mostly repeated patterns or led to inaccessible,
duplicate or ineligible sources. Provider credentials, registration,
experience, availability and competence claims remain provider
self-description unless an official source is explicitly cited.

### Learning-market sample

Thirteen pages across 12 United States, United Kingdom, Canadian and New
Zealand domains were purposively selected for their page structures,
terminology, taxonomy, eligibility, trust and service-boundary patterns. This
is a pattern sample, not a representative market comparison. No foreign traffic,
conversion, price or outcome data was available.

### Comparison rules

- Search-result pages, provider units, practitioners, directory profiles and
  clients are different units and are never added together.
- Search composition and provider supply are not demand.
- `Not observed` means only that the sampled accessible page did not display
  the item.
- Fees retain their visible duration, configuration, clinician, concession,
  room-fee and rebate conditions. Non-equivalent units are not averaged.
- Directory counts describe the platform response and inclusion rules on the
  observation date, not verified specialists or market size.
- Evidence, inference, assumptions and recommendations are labelled separately
  in consequential sections.

### What the query sample supports

**Evidence:** `ENM`, `polyamory`, `non-monogamy`, `open relationships` and
`relationship anarchy` all occur in the visible result ecosystem. The terms do
not produce equivalent result compositions. `Open relationship counselling
Perth` was especially noisy, while national polyamory and online
non-monogamy queries returned many direct-provider pages. [Q01-Q08]

**Inference:** A single page should cover a recognisable term family rather
than betting on one acronym or building one URL per synonym. `Polyamory` and
the full phrases `ethical non-monogamy` or `consensual non-monogamy` deserve
prominence; `open relationships` should remain plain-language supporting
coverage rather than the only SEO proposition. `Relationship anarchy` is
useful secondary coverage to test, not a proven primary acquisition term.

**Recommendation:** Use a clear Australia/Perth geographic signal in title,
introductory copy and service facts. The Scotland ambiguity in Q03 is not a
finding about Google, but it strengthens the case for writing `Perth, Western
Australia` or `Perth WA` rather than relying on `Perth` alone. Validate the
fixed query set in an actual Perth Google and Maps environment after launch.

## Demand: what is and is not known

### Direct segment demand

No authorised first-party query, impression, click, enquiry, booking, capacity
or revenue data was available. Google Keyword Planner and comparable paid
keyword datasets were unavailable; the in-app browser had no active session,
so a defensible Google Trends comparison could not be collected. [D01]

This means:

- the research cannot estimate Australian or Perth search volume;
- result counts, provider counts and directory totals cannot be substituted for
  demand;
- the segment should not be described as high demand, fast growing, underserved
  or commercially validated; and
- the absence of measured demand is not evidence of zero or low demand.

### Broad service context

ABS Patient Experiences 2024-25 reported that 17.3% of people saw a health
professional for their own mental health in the preceding 12 months. Within a
combined `other mental health professional` category that includes counsellors,
social workers and several other roles, 2.3% saw such a professional. Among
people who needed and saw a mental-health professional, 26.0% used telehealth
at least once. Cost caused 19.7% to delay or not see any mental-health
professional and 17.0% to delay or not see the combined other-professional
category. These are broad access conditions, not ENM demand estimates. [M01]

Healthdirect describes counselling and psychotherapy as support delivered by
counsellors and psychotherapists and notes that counselling sessions are not
covered by Medicare. Services Australia's Better Access information lists
eligible psychologists and certain social workers and occupational therapists;
it does not make counsellors eligible providers. Role, referral, price and
rebate clarity therefore matter in the comparison set, but should be stated
without implying that every provider or client uses the same funding pathway.
[M02-M03]

### Commercial interpretation

**Inference:** The existing draft page makes a market test relatively cheap and
reversible. Vive does not need proof of a large market before exposing an
accurate page, because the implementation cost is much lower than launching a
new service line or paid campaign.

**Recommendation:** Treat the first release as an instrumented discovery test.
Do not forecast lead volume or change pricing/capacity on the basis of this
dossier alone.

### Supply patterns that matter to Vive

- All 16 sampled providers offer individual work. Fourteen also offer couples
  or relationship work; five explicitly describe a three-or-more format.
  Individual counselling is therefore a normal segment offer, but it must be
  made explicit because many competing pages also imply relationship sessions.
  [C01-C16]
- Eight of 16 explicitly describe Australia-wide online delivery. Perth
  prospects can therefore compare local practitioners with interstate online
  providers, while Vive can compete for suitable clients beyond WA. [C03-C04;
  C08; C10-C11; C13; C15-C16]
- Five providers make ENM/polyamory a primary brand or dedicated-page subject;
  11 present it within a wider practice. Both page models are visibly viable;
  public performance data was not available to compare them. [C01-C16]
- Professional labels include counsellor, psychologist, psychotherapist,
  social worker, sexologist, sex therapist and combinations. The visitor is
  comparing more than ENM stance: role, configuration, method, delivery, price,
  rebate and trust signals are part of the offer. [C01-C16; M02-M03]

## Contradictions, absences and unresolved questions

- **Dedicated versus broad presentation:** both models appear locally and
  overseas. No source exposed traffic or conversion data, so this dossier does
  not claim dedicated pages always perform better. Vive already has a relevant
  route, making completion the practical choice.
- **Affirming versus specialist:** providers use `friendly`, `affirming`,
  `aware`, `allied`, `informed`, `experienced` and `specialist` without a common
  demonstrated standard. Vive should substantiate its own wording rather than
  adopt the strongest available label.
- **ENM versus CNM:** both appear, sometimes as synonyms. They are not
  linguistically identical, and no volume or audience-preference evidence
  selects one winner. Use the term family accurately and learn from real
  queries.
- **Individual versus relationship work:** individual work is common, yet many
  offers also serve couples or 3+ configurations. A visitor can reasonably be
  uncertain what `ENM counselling` means unless attendance and scope are stated.
- **Relationship structure versus identity:** providers variously frame ENM as
  identity, orientation, choice, lifestyle, model, structure or context. This
  market research cannot decide the client's preferred framing. Avoid imposing
  one account on everyone.
- **Local familiarity versus boundary conflict:** being locally known may help
  trust and may disqualify a prospective client. Marketing must not promise that
  every community member can be accepted.
- **Directory count versus usable supply:** directory results mix local,
  online-only, serves-area, different professions and self-selected categories.
  Counts cannot establish specialist capacity.
- **Price versus total access:** visible fees do not capture availability,
  waitlists, rebate eligibility, fit, duration of therapy or concession access.
- **No performance data:** provider pages and foreign models disclose no
  rankings, traffic, enquiries, bookings, retention or outcomes.
- **No audience research:** provider language shows how services present the
  market, not how Australian ENM clients describe lived experience or choose a
  counsellor. That belongs in the separate audience-research workflow.

## What this means for Vive

### 1. The opportunity is an accurate discovery asset, not a new broad service line

**Evidence:** Direct providers, topic directories and national-online offers are
visible across the query set. Demand volume remains unmeasured. Vive already
has a substantial but hidden draft route. [Q01-Q08; C01-C16; D01; project
current scope]

**Interpretation:** Publishing an accurate page is a low-cost, reversible way to
test whether the segment produces appropriate enquiries. Building a content
vertical, changing the core offer or forecasting a large revenue stream would
run ahead of the evidence.

**Recommendation:** Complete and expose one canonical page, then let impressions,
queries and enquiries determine whether the segment deserves more investment.

### 2. The offer should lead with individual depth and non-steering support

**Evidence:** Every sampled provider offers individuals, but most also offer
relationships, and many visible propositions use interchangeable affirming
language. [C01-C16]

**Interpretation:** `ENM-friendly counselling` alone is too vague to explain why
someone should choose Vive or what they can book. Joel's distinctive fit is the
combination of individual work, psychodynamic/attachment depth, relationship-
structure familiarity and a stance that does not decide the right structure for
the client.

**Recommendation:** Make the page answer, early and explicitly:

- this is counselling for one adult at a time;
- you may come because ENM is the central issue, relevant context, something you
  are exploring, or simply part of your life;
- the work can include attachment, jealousy, rupture, shame, grief, identity,
  boundaries, communication patterns and competing needs without assuming ENM
  caused them; and
- counselling will not steer the person toward monogamy or non-monogamy.

These are proposition decisions. Exact visitor-facing language still belongs
in the copy-development workflow.

### 3. Perth and Australia-wide are one service proposition with different jobs

**Evidence:** Perth-visible supply exists, national online supply is common, and
local community overlap can make some prospects inappropriate for Joel. [Q01-
Q08; C03-C04; C08; C10-C11; C13; C15-C16; owner context]

**Interpretation:** Perth establishes local relevance and in-person access;
Australia-wide online delivery creates usable reach and helps compensate for a
small overlapping local network. This is not a reason to create near-duplicate
location pages.

**Recommendation:** Use one canonical page with a clear Perth WA and online-
Australia service statement. Handle known-person conflicts privately and
respectfully in the suitability process. Maintain a referral map for unsuitable
local overlaps and unsupported service configurations.

### 4. Trust must combine familiarity with professional specificity

**Evidence:** The market uses many unstandardised inclusion labels and multiple
professional roles. Directories often rely on self-selection. [C01-C16; T01-
T09]

**Interpretation:** Lived familiarity can reduce the fear of having to educate a
counsellor, but an unexplained insider claim can feel vague or create boundary
questions. Credential-only messaging also fails to demonstrate relationship-
diversity understanding.

**Recommendation:** Pair restrained familiarity language with Joel's counsellor
role, current professional membership/qualification, psychodynamic and
attachment-informed approach, individual scope, confidentiality, service area,
fee and honest limits. Avoid `expert`, `specialist` or competence-superlative
language unless separately substantiated and approved.

### 5. Search language should aid recognition, not make the page read like a glossary

**Evidence:** Query and provider terminology form a family, while exact query
performance varies. Overseas pages show deeper vocabularies but no Australian
performance data. [Q01-Q08; C01-C26]

**Interpretation:** Plain phrasing lets both community-fluent people and those
without acronyms recognise the service. Excessive insider vocabulary risks
performative copy and informational traffic outside the actual offer.

**Recommendation:** Use `ethical non-monogamy (ENM)`, `consensual
non-monogamy`, `polyamory` and `open relationships` naturally. Use
`relationship anarchy` where relevant as supporting coverage. Add deeper terms
only where they explain genuine counselling situations, and use Search Console
and enquiry language before expanding into support content.

### 6. Clear access facts can outperform vague comprehensiveness

**Evidence:** Public offers differ in price, duration, configuration, delivery,
consultation and rebate position. Vive's A$120/50-minute fee is accessible
within the comparable visible sample, but no price leadership can be claimed.
[C01-C16; M02-M03]

**Interpretation:** A visitor comparing unlike provider types needs operational
clarity, especially because psychologist rebate pathways may be visible nearby.

**Recommendation:** Put the A$120/50-minute fee, free 15-minute consultation,
no-referral requirement, rebate position, Perth/in-person and Australia/online
availability, individual-only format and enquiry process in one easily scanned
service-facts area.

## Prioritised recommendations

### Now: complete the minimum credible market test

1. **Finish the existing ENM/polyamory page as one canonical service page.**
   Resolve the individual-service boundary, factual service details and final
   copy; then remove noindex/nofollow, include it in the sitemap and connect it
   through an appropriate inclusion/service route. Do this only after the page
   accurately represents the offer.
2. **Build the proposition around individual counselling, depth and
   non-steering support.** Cover the term family but write for recognition and
   fit rather than keyword repetition.
3. **Make the dual geography explicit.** State Perth, Western Australia for
   local/in-person relevance and online counselling across Australia for the
   serviceable market. Keep one URL.
4. **Publish operational facts together.** Individual format, fee, duration,
   consultation, referral/rebate position, online/in-person modes, enquiry path
   and any current eligibility limits should be immediately findable.
5. **Instrument discovery and enquiry.** Establish Search Console coverage and
   privacy-safe analytics before or at indexation so the test does not start
   blind.
6. **Create a private referral and boundary process.** Cover couples/polycules,
   known community members, conflicts, unavailable capacity and needs outside
   scope. Research inclusion is not a provider endorsement; verify any referral
   independently.

### Next: test distribution and learn from real behaviour

1. Review the fixed query set from a real Perth Google and Maps environment,
   recording ads, map pack, organic pages, directory presence and date.
2. If eligible and affordable, test one Psychology Today profile/category
   placement with tracked attribution. Assess Come Back to Bed separately for
   topic-specific discovery or referrals.
3. Review Search Console query wording, page impressions, clicks and geography
   after the page has had time to be crawled. Review appropriate enquiry quality
   alongside volume.
4. Collect a minimal private source code for enquiries: organic search,
   directory name, referral, direct/returning, or other. Do not send sensitive
   relationship details to analytics platforms.
5. Refine title, meta description, internal anchor text, FAQ coverage and
   geographic wording only after evidence shows a recognition or intent gap.

### Later: invest only when observed demand earns it

1. Consider one supporting resource only if Search Console or repeated
   enquiries reveal a specific question the service page cannot answer cleanly.
2. Consider paid search only after organic enquiry quality, capacity, negative
   keywords, conversion tracking and an acceptable acquisition threshold are
   understood.
3. Revisit pricing only with capacity, conversion, affordability and client-fit
   evidence; do not react to a small visible competitor sample.
4. Refresh the query, provider, directory and fee observations before a major
   positioning or budget decision, because these are volatile.

## What not to do yet

- Do not create separate thin pages for ENM, CNM, polyamory, open relationships,
  relationship anarchy, Perth and Australia.
- Do not launch suburb doorway pages or pretend a Perth query proves demand in
  each suburb.
- Do not build a glossary/content hub merely because mature overseas sites have
  one.
- Do not call the segment high demand, rapidly growing, underserved or
  low-competition without direct evidence.
- Do not use directory totals as provider or client counts.
- Do not imply couples, polycule or group counselling when the offer is
  individual work.
- Do not make foreign licensing, insurance, accreditation, prices or worldwide
  service claims part of Australian positioning.
- Do not make active promotion inside a small Perth ENM community the primary
  acquisition plan where dual-role and known-person risks are material.
- Do not share sensitive relationship structure, enquiry text, form values or
  identifiable clinical context with general analytics or advertising tools.
- Do not rank or recommend sampled providers on the basis of public pages.

## Measurement and decision plan

### Instrumentation

| Question | Measure | Implementation note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Can search engines discover the page? | Index status, sitemap inclusion, canonical, crawl errors | Search Console URL inspection and coverage |
| Which language creates visibility? | Impressions, clicks, CTR and average position by query and page | Search Console; group terms without hiding exact-query detail |
| Does geography match the offer? | Country and broad region where safely available; enquiry source/location stated voluntarily | Avoid precise sensitive profiling; compare Perth/WA and rest of Australia carefully |
| Does the page support action? | Page view, enquiry-start and enquiry-success events | Events should contain page/CTA identifiers only, never free-text or relationship details |
| Which channel contributes? | UTM/source code for each directory and campaign; self-reported source | Use one distinguishable code per listing; do not rely only on last-click analytics |
| Are enquiries suitable? | Private owner-coded outcome: suitable/unsuitable/unknown and broad reason | Keep outside public analytics; use a deliberately small, non-clinical taxonomy |
| Is the service commercially useful? | Appropriate enquiries, consultations, bookings and capacity consumed | Review counts and qualitative fit; avoid conversion-rate precision with tiny samples |

### Baseline and cadence

Before indexation, record the current zero/hidden baseline, current route state,
fee, capacity, query snapshots and directory listings. After launch:

- verify indexation and event operation in the first two weeks;
- check for technical failure without judging demand too early;
- perform the first organic decision review after approximately 12 weeks of
  successful indexation;
- continue monthly lightweight checks of queries, appropriate enquiries and
  capacity; and
- make the first support-content or paid-channel decision at roughly six months,
  unless evidence or capacity changes materially sooner.

### Decision rules

- **Continue:** the page earns relevant impressions or suitable enquiries at a
  manageable level, even if total volume is modest.
- **Refine:** impressions are relevant but CTR is weak; clicks occur but service
  configuration is misunderstood; or enquiries reveal a recurring answerable
  fit question.
- **Expand cautiously:** repeated suitable enquiries or query clusters show one
  clearly adjacent content need and Vive has capacity.
- **Do not expand:** traffic is mostly informational or geographically
  ineligible, enquiries repeatedly require unsupported couples/polycule work,
  or capacity/boundary constraints outweigh value.
- **Reassess the proposition:** after a technically sound six-month test the
  page has negligible relevant impressions and no other referral value.

No minimum enquiry target is invented here. A target should reflect Vive's
available client capacity and acceptable acquisition effort, which were not
quantified in this research.

## Decision summary

**Recommended decision:** Complete, index and measure one Australian ENM and
polyamory individual-counselling page, with Perth WA and Australia-wide online
delivery expressed on the same canonical URL.

**Why:** The category has visible provider and intermediary supply, the current
draft makes testing relatively cheap, and Vive has a plausible differentiated
fit. Direct demand remains unmeasured, so the appropriate commitment is an
instrumented page and limited channel test rather than a content programme or
large acquisition spend.

**Proposition to test:** Individual psychodynamic and attachment-informed
counselling where ENM/polyamory can be the issue, context, exploration or
background, without steering the person toward a preferred relationship
structure.

**Highest-risk assumptions:** that acquisition is the goal, individual-only
scope remains correct, and Australia-wide capacity is operationally available.

**First validation:** confirm service facts and boundary; index the finished
page; measure queries and privacy-safe enquiry actions; then judge appropriate
enquiries over approximately 12 weeks and broader investment at six months.
