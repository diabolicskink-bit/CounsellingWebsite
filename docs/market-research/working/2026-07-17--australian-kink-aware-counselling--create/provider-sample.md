# Australian kink-aware counselling and therapy: purposive provider sample

Observed 17 July 2026. This is a visibility-and-diversity sample, not a census, market share estimate, quality assessment, or recommendation list. Provider roles, credentials, modalities, community affiliations, and clinical claims below are self-descriptions on the cited pages.

## Scope and method

- **Inclusion:** an Australian service serving adults whose current first-party page explicitly used `kink`, `BDSM`, `fetish`, `D/s`, `power exchange`, or a close equivalent in connection with counselling, psychology, psychotherapy, sex therapy, sexology, or relationship work. Both in-person Australian services and services explicitly offered online to Australian adults were eligible.
- **Direct versus adjacent:** the direct sample contains counselling, psychology, psychotherapy, relationship therapy, and sex-therapy/sexology offers. Coaching, professional education, workshops, and experiential BDSM services are reported separately even when their language overlaps with therapy.
- **Sampled unit:** one distinct public-facing provider brand or practice. Multiple pages and clinicians were consolidated into that brand; a clinician is named in the record where the explicit kink language was clinician-specific. This avoids counting one clinic, clinician profile, service page, and directory profile as separate providers.
- **Discovery:** exact searches were `kink friendly therapist Australia`, `BDSM counselling Australia`, `kink aware psychologist Australia`, `D/s relationship counselling Australia`, `kink friendly therapist Perth`, and `kink counselling online Australia`. Directory leads were checked from [Psychology Today’s Australian sex-positive/kink-allied filter](https://www.psychologytoday.com/au/counselling?category=sex-positive-kink-allied), its [Perth/WA filter](https://www.psychologytoday.com/au/counselling/wa/perth?category=sex-positive-kink-allied), and the [Kink Aware Professionals Australia directory](https://www.kapprofessionals.org/kap_directory/wpbdm-region/australia/?wpbdp_sort=-field-modified). Directory and search snippets were used for discovery only; record-level findings come from the original pages listed under each provider.
- **Search surface:** searches were run through a general web-search API, not a reproducible Google results page. Ranking depth, personalisation, precise search location, and index completeness were not visible. Results therefore indicate discoverability on the observation date, not demand or rank.
- **Stopping rule:** sampling stopped when the six exact queries, the two geographic filters, and focused follow-up checks were mostly returning already sampled brands, directory-only profiles, inaccessible pages, or additional providers repeating the role/location/offer patterns already represented. The final sample deliberately preserves variety across Perth/WA and national online supply, practitioner categories, practice configurations, page presentation, and price disclosure.
- **Evidence limits:** `not visible on sampled pages` means only that the item was not visible on the pages checked. Dynamic booking portals were not treated as evidence when their contents did not render. Public prices are transcribed without averaging because provider, duration, configuration, concession, rebate, and tax bases are not consistently comparable.

## Cross-sample findings

- The direct sample contains **19 provider brands**, including **7 based in Perth or regional WA**. Six brands explicitly described unqualified Australia-wide online reach; PolyPsychology described online reach across Australia while expressly excluding Victoria.
- Only **2 of 19** direct brands gave kink/BDSM a dedicated first-party destination: Progressive Therapeutic Collective’s kink/BDSM service page and SHIPS Psychology’s broader support page for erotically marginalised communities. The other 17 placed the language on a homepage, clinician biography, general services list, relationship page, FAQ, or inclusion statement. Explicit visibility therefore often depended on finding a subsection rather than a primary navigation destination.
- The labels were not standardised. Visible formulations included `kink-friendly`, `kink-aware`, `kink allied`, `kink informed`, `kink/BDSM savvy`, `sex-positive/kink allied`, `kink-identified`, and named `Kink & BDSM Counselling`. `D/s` or power-exchange language was materially less common on direct-service pages than general `kink` or `BDSM`; Progressive Therapeutic Collective was the clearest direct-service example. `Fetish` appeared explicitly at Deeper Roots, Safari Lee, and Laura Davis.
- Adjacent positioning frequently clustered around LGBTQIA+/queer and trans inclusion, consensual or ethical non-monogamy/polyamory, sex-work inclusion, and neurodivergence. These clusters were recorded as provider language, not evidence that every provider has equivalent competence across all named communities.
- **14 of 19** direct brands disclosed at least one public price on a sampled page; **12** paired at least one price with a duration. Price disclosure ranged from a single standard appointment to clinician-by-clinician matrices. Two brands had internal page inconsistencies: Corina Johns Psychology showed $200 on the services page and $190 on the about page; Progressive Therapeutic Collective showed a $105 accessibility price on its kink page and $115 on its general price page.
- Practice configurations extended beyond an individual/couple binary. Several pages explicitly named polycules, throuples, groups, multiple simultaneous partners, or sessions for three or four people. Other brands limited relationship work to two people or did not make configuration visible.
- The supply boundary is porous. Psychologists and counselling clinics sit beside sexologists, psychosexual therapists, somatic sex therapists, relationship counsellors, and mixed counselling/coaching practices. Pleasure Centred Sexology explicitly states that its work is not medical or clinical even though it offers somatic sex therapy; PolyPsychology explicitly blends counselling and coaching. The adjacent sample makes this boundary more visible rather than collapsing all offers into therapy.
