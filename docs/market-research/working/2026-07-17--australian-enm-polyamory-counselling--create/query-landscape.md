This packet records a dated, fixed-depth sample. It does not assign canonical
source IDs and must not be treated as an indexed dossier on its own.

## Scope and boundary

Included as a **direct provider** only when the visible result title or extract
showed an Australian adult individual counselling, psychology, psychotherapy or
sexology service that publicly addressed ethical or consensual non-monogamy,
ENM/CNM, polyamory, open relationships or relationship anarchy. A service could
also see couples or multiple partners and remain direct if individual work was
visible.

The other coding values were:

- **Adjacent provider/service:** couples-only or multi-partner-only therapy,
  coaching, practitioner supervision, education or another overlapping offer.
- **Directory/intermediary:** a directory, association, marketplace or hosted
  practitioner profile. These remain intermediary pages even when the extract
  describes a relevant practitioner.
- **Informational/publisher:** a general information, professional education or
  publisher page rather than a consumer provider or directory.
- **Community:** a community or peer-group result.
- **Irrelevant/ambiguous:** the visible result did not establish the defined
  ENM segment, used `open` in another sense, or otherwise appeared to match only
  a generic part of the query.

Provider geography and serviceability are recorded separately from result
type. Thus, a UK directory about Perth, Scotland is still coded as a directory,
but is marked not relevant to the Australian delivery market.

## Method and access limits

Eight exact queries supplied by the parent were each issued separately. The
first 10 general link results returned for each dedicated single-query call
were retained, giving 80 page-level observations. A preliminary combined-query
call was used to inspect the interface but is excluded because its output did
not preserve a clear query boundary.

The search tool exposed link results, titles, URLs and crawled extracts. It did
not expose a named search engine, ads, maps/local packs, autocomplete, related
searches, question features, device, account state, safe-search state,
personalisation controls or an actual search-location setting. The session
timezone was Australia/Perth, but that does **not** establish that the search
engine observed the query from Perth. Accordingly:

- query geography is only the place written in each query;
- search-observation geography is unknown/generic;
- result geography is inferred only from the visible title, URL or extract;
- result order is a one-time interface order, not a stable rank;
- no result was counted as evidence of search volume, demand, market share,
  audience preference, provider quality or service uptake; and
- classifications describe what was visible in the result extract. Pages were
  not comprehensively audited for services that the extract did not show.

The extracts had crawl-freshness labels ranging from the observation day to
several months. The sample date is the date the result was observed, not
necessarily the date each underlying page changed.

Major repeat visibility explains some of the page-level totals:

- Psychology Today occupied 11/40 Perth-written result slots, often through
  several category URL variants. It occupied 4/40 Australia-written slots.
- ENM Australia occupied 6/40 Australia-written slots across home, services
  and about pages. All were coded adjacent because the visible offer was

  clinical-supervision pages; only the supervision page was adjacent.
- Davina Morley appeared through direct home/contact/services pages and a
  Melbourne EFT profile. The provider identity is one even when the host or
  page differs.
- Progressive Therapeutic Collective, Mark Roussel Counselling, the UK
  Counselling Directory and several Psychology Today category variants also
  produced duplicate page visibility.

Directory-hosted practitioner profiles were not converted into direct-provider
pages. They can reveal a provider identity, but their page role in search is
still intermediary visibility. Likewise, no attempt was made to infer that two
similarly named people or brands were identical without a clear result-level
link.

## Evidence-bounded findings

### The four written Perth terms behaved very differently

Within this interface, `polyamory` and `non monogamy` returned visibly relevant
provider and directory pages, while `open relationship` was highly ambiguous.
Eight of the 10 `open relationship counselling Perth` results were generic
relationship or couples pages with no consensual-open-relationship relevance
visible. Several appeared to match language such as `open communication` or
simply the separate words `relationship`, `counselling` and `Perth`.

The acronym query `ENM counselling Perth` produced relevant direct providers,
but its top 10 were split between directory variants (4), direct providers (2)
and generic Perth counselling pages (4). This establishes mixed visible result
composition, not weak or strong demand.

### `Perth` created a real geographic ambiguity

Two of the first 10 results for `non monogamy therapist Perth` were separate URL
variants of a UK directory category for Perth, Scotland (PH postcodes). This is
direct evidence that adding `Perth` without `WA`, `Western Australia` or
`Australia` does not force this generic interface to the target city. It does
not establish how Google in Perth would resolve the same query.

### National, serviceable-market queries exposed more direct-provider pages

In the four Australia-written samples, 21/40 page results were direct-provider
pages versus 11/40 in the Perth-written samples. After within-sample provider
deduplication, the corresponding labelled direct-provider identities were 12
and seven. This is a visibility-composition contrast, not a supply census or a
demand comparison.

The densest single sample was `polyamory therapist Australia` with 7/10 direct
provider pages (six provider identities). `non monogamy counselling online
Australia` had 6/10 direct pages (five identities) and also surfaced an
Australian clinician directory and practitioner education. The explicit
`online Australia` construction aligned closely with providers that stated
national telehealth eligibility.

### The result vocabulary forms a family, not a single interchangeable term

Across relevant results, providers and directories visibly used:

- `ethical non-monogamy` and the acronym `ENM`;
- `consensual non-monogamy` and `CNM`;
- `polyamory`, `poly`, `polyamorous` and `poly-friendly`;
- `open relationships` or `open dynamics`;
- `relationship anarchy` and `RA`; and
- configuration labels including individual, couple/partners, triad/throuple,
  polycule, group or wider relationship network.

The sampled result sets show that all four concept families can retrieve
Australian provider pages. They do not establish which term Australians use
most, which term converts, or which label the intended audience prefers.

### Role and offer boundaries are visible in search

The national acronym sample mixed consumer counselling with coaching,
sexology, practitioner supervision, an association and general health
information. ENM Australia's multiple visible pages demonstrate that a highly
query-aligned brand may still be an adjacent coaching offer rather than a
like-for-like counselling provider. Conversely, directories and hosted
profiles may dominate page visibility without representing separate provider
businesses.

### Dedicated and broad pages both appeared

Dedicated topic URLs were visible for ENM counselling, polyamory counselling,
ethical non-monogamy and non-monogamy/relationship-anarchy counselling. Broad
home pages and practitioner profile pages also appeared when their titles or
extracts carried the terminology. The sample therefore supports the existence
of both topic-page and broad-positioning models; it does not compare their SEO
performance.

## Contradictions, sampled absences and cautions

- `open relationship counselling Perth` looks semantically direct to a human,
  yet the sampled interface mostly treated `open` as ordinary relationship or
  communication language. This contradicts any assumption that the phrase is
  automatically a precise ENM discovery query.
- Exact `ENM` did not eliminate generic counselling results. On the national
  query it also surfaced several adjacent coaching/supervision pages.
- The local sample did not surface an independent direct-provider page for the
  `open relationship` query, although clearly relevant providers appeared for
  the other Perth terms. This is an absence from one 10-result sample, not from
  Perth or the web.
- No community result appeared in the first 10 of any sampled query. Community
  and Reddit results occurred below the retained depth on some responses, so
  the correct finding is only `0/80 at this fixed depth`.
- No keyword-volume, Google Trends, Search Console, advertising or enquiry data
  was available in this stream. Search demand remains unmeasured.
- No map pack, ad, autocomplete, question feature or Google-specific local
  surface was available. Local SEO conclusions require a real Perth-located
  Google observation and, ideally, first-party Search Console/enquiry data.
- Some result extracts were rich enough to show delivery and configuration;
  others were not. `Not visible` must not be converted into `not offered`.

## Evidence-bounded implications for integration

These are candidate implications for the parent to combine with provider,
demand, owner and broader-market evidence—not final recommendations.

1. A Perth-facing discovery strategy should probably not rely on `open
   relationship counselling` as its only or primary precision phrase. The
   exact local sample was dominated by generic relationship pages. ENM,
   polyamory and non-monogamy each retrieved more visibly relevant pages in
   this interface.
2. It is reasonable to test a terminology cluster rather than a single label:
   `ENM / ethical non-monogamy`, `polyamory`, `consensual non-monogamy`, `open
   relationships` and, where substantively relevant, `relationship anarchy`.
   This is a coverage hypothesis; Australian search volumes and conversion are
   still unknown.
3. Perth wording should be disambiguated with `WA`, `Western Australia` or
   Australia context where the page/title can do so naturally. The Scotland
   result collision makes this a search-clarity issue, although a real Perth
   Google SERP still needs checking.
4. National online eligibility is not merely background: Australia-written
   queries visibly returned multiple direct providers whose pages explicitly
   state online Australia-wide service. A parent recommendation can treat
   these as serviceable-market competition, while keeping non-serviceable and
   directory-only pages separate.
5. Clear role and configuration language would help distinguish individual
   counselling from couples-only therapy, coaching, multi-person support,
   sexology, education and clinician supervision. Search visibility alone does
   not tell the visitor which offer is appropriate.
6. Psychology Today and the Australian ENM clinician directory are search
   intermediaries worth examining in the separate directory/provider stream.
   Their presence here establishes visibility, not endorsement or completeness.
7. A dedicated topic page is a plausible model because multiple Australian
   providers surfaced with dedicated ENM/poly/non-monogamy URLs. Whether the
   owner should create or reshape one depends on current site structure,
   substantive scope, keyword data and copy strategy.

## Integration cautions

- Retain all counts as `first 10 generic web results observed 2026-07-17`, not
  as Google positions or market shares.
- Keep page-level visibility and distinct provider identity separate.
- Keep Perth/WA target evidence, Australia-wide online serviceability and the
  Scotland false-positive separate.
- Do not use the greater national direct-provider share as evidence of greater
  national demand.
- Do not use result language as evidence of audience preference.
- Re-open original provider/directory pages before integrating exact prices,
  credentials, availability, directory counts or consequential provider facts;
  this packet's classifications are based on the search observation itself.
