# Provider and directory market packet: Australian ENM/polyamory counselling

**Status:** Delegated working evidence; non-canonical. The parent agent must
verify consequential observations, resolve duplicates, and assign final source
IDs before integration.

**Observation date:** 2026-07-17

## Research frame

### Question

What counselling, psychology, psychotherapy, social-work and sexology supply is
publicly visible to an adult in Perth/WA or available online across Australia;
how do those providers present ENM/polyamory work; and which directories and
professional taxonomies shape discovery?

### Geographic roles

- **Target decision market:** Perth and Western Australia. Local office/base
  and local in-person access are treated as target-market facts.
- **Serviceable market:** Australia-wide online services. An interstate
  provider enters the direct sample only when its own page says that online
  access reaches Australia, or when its publicly maintained provider profile
  makes the relevant service area explicit.
- **Comparable market:** other Australian cities, principally Melbourne and
  Sydney, used here to show national-online role and offer variation. This
  packet does not convert their office locations or prices into Perth facts.
- **No learning-market providers** are included in the direct sample.

### Inclusion and exclusion boundary

The direct sample includes an adult individual counselling, psychology,
psychotherapy, social-work or sexology service whose sampled page or provider
profile explicitly names ethical/consensual non-monogamy, ENM/CNM, polyamory,
open relationships or relationship anarchy. Every included service offers
individual work; relationship work may also be offered.

The following remain separate:

- couples-only, multi-partner-only and peer-group offers;
- coaching, education, courses and community support without counselling or
  psychotherapy;
- directories and association/member taxonomies; and
- general relationship or sex-therapy pages that did not visibly name the
  segment on the sampled page.

`Provider` is a public-facing practice or solo practitioner, not a quality
judgement. `Direct provider unit` is the distinct brand/practice used for the
comparison. Team practices therefore count once even when several clinicians
appear. `Dedicated/high-emphasis` means the segment is a primary page, headline
or brand proposition; `inclusion mention` means it appears within a broader
service or profile. None of these labels establishes competence, outcomes,
availability or client preference.

### Discovery and stopping rule

Discovery used repeated generic web searches for combinations of `Perth`,
`Australia`, `online`, `counselling`, `psychologist`, `therapist`, `sexologist`,
`ENM`, `ethical non-monogamy`, `polyamory`, `open relationships` and
`relationship anarchy`; the Psychology Today Perth category; the Come Back to
Bed clinician directory; and the Australian KAP region page. Provider facts and
exact prices were then checked on the provider's own site wherever accessible.

The sample was purposive, not exhaustive. It includes seven clearly surfaced
WA-based units and nine national-online/comparable-city units, for 16 distinct
direct provider units. Searching stopped when new results predominantly repeated
the represented role/offer combinations or fell outside the boundary. Examples
excluded at this stage were Hanieh Tolouei and Wellbeing Counsellor (adult
relationship/sex services, but no ENM/poly term was observed on the sampled
pages), Bronwyn Singleton Therapy (Toronto, Canada), and ENM Australia
(explicitly coaching rather than psychotherapy).

Two included records are provider-maintained directory profiles rather than an
accessible first-party site page: Corina Johns on KAP (the linked site would not
load in the research interface) and Sofia Kouznetsova on Psychology Today. This
is identified in the rows and limits the comparison.

### What the directory layer adds

1. **Directories define the segment differently.** Psychology Today treats
   `Open Relationships Non-Monogamy` as a gender/sexuality filter; KAP embeds
   poly awareness inside a much broader kink/non-monogamy professional
   directory; Come Back to Bed uses self-identified “allied clinician”; SAS
   organises professional role/accreditation without an ENM issue field; and
   SSEAA combines poly/open-relationship interest with coaching, bodywork and
   education. These taxonomies are not interchangeable.
2. **Location filters often mix office and reach.** The Perth Psychology Today
   page includes local offices, online-only and “serves area” cards. Come Back
   to Bed separates states and online-only, but the online section itself
   includes an overseas provider and a WA-based provider. A directory count
   cannot be read as local bricks-and-mortar supply without row-level coding.
3. **Credential and ENM signals are separate fields.** Psychology Today can
   verify a primary credential; SAS can signal sexology accreditation; KAP and
   Come Back to Bed signal self-described awareness/alliance. None of those
   alone establishes provider quality, treatment outcomes or issue-specific
   competence.
4. **The visible directory pool is much broader than the explicit-copy pool.**
   Only 4 of the 20 extracted first-page Psychology Today cards named the
   segment in their visible excerpt. This does not mean the other 16 are not
   allied; it means category metadata and explicit presentation are distinct.

## Evidence-bounded synthesis

### 1. Perth supply is visible, but it is fragmented across professional roles

Seven of the 16 direct provider units are WA-based. Five of those seven visibly
offer an in-person option (Sara Leopard, Sex Therapy Perth, Jessica Salomone,
Corina Johns and Sofia Kouznetsova); two are Perth-based telehealth practices
(Dan Ward and Kendall Buckley). The small sample spans psychology, social work,
counselling, psychotherapy and sexology, including solo and team practices.

This supports a limited conclusion: a Perth adult can find explicitly
poly/ENM-aware services without relying only on an interstate provider. It does
not establish availability, wait time, competence, market share or that these
are all local providers.

### 2. Australia-wide telehealth changes who competes for a Perth individual

Eight of 16 units explicitly say that their online work reaches clients across
Australia: Dan Ward, Kendall Buckley, Floraison, PolyPsychology (excluding
Victoria), MoonGrove, George Klein (for individuals), Tania Rose and Love
Therapy Australia. Several other units offer online work but did not state the
eligible geography on the sampled page, so they were not added to that count.

The visible serviceable set is therefore broader than the Perth office set.
Office address alone would understate substitution for an individual willing to
use telehealth. Conversely, a generic `online` label should not be treated as
Australia-wide access when the page does not say so.

### 3. Individual support is a normal offer, not merely a route into couples therapy

All 16 direct provider units offer individual work by design; 14 also visibly
offer couples/relationship work. Five explicitly name a 3+ form such as
polycules, poly families, throuples, groups or relationships of 3+. Dan Ward and
Maya Floyd are the two sampled units whose relevant pages describe individual
work but not relationship sessions.

This market presentation supports keeping individual, two-person and 3+
configurations analytically separate. It would be inaccurate to describe the
visible segment as couples counselling only, but equally inaccurate to assume
that every provider can convene a whole polycule.

### 4. Two presentation models recur

Five units make ENM/polyamory a primary brand or page proposition: Floraison,
Evolve Together, PolyPsychology, MoonGrove and Love In Mind. The remaining 11
use explicit inclusion within a broader mental-health, sexology, relationship
or practitioner page. These are presentation codes within this sample, not
competence codes.

The pages also reveal two overlapping service models:

- **ENM as the presenting issue:** opening a relationship, jealousy,
  agreements, communication, boundaries or multi-partner dynamics; and
- **ENM as ordinary client context:** anxiety, depression, trauma, identity,
  life change or other counselling where the provider says the relationship
  structure need not be explained or pathologised.

Corina Johns states this distinction explicitly, while Dan Ward and Maya Floyd
place ENM within general individual work. The dedicated practices more often
pair service pages with guides, blogs, books or free resources. That indicates
different acquisition and positioning models, not different quality levels.

### 5. The language family is broader than the acronym `ENM`

Across the sample, providers repeatedly use a cluster: `ethical non-monogamy`,
`consensual non-monogamy`, `ENM/CNM`, `polyamory`, and `open relationships`.
`Relationship anarchy`, `swinging`, and `polycules/throuples` appear less
universally. Kink/BDSM, queer/LGBTQIA+ and neurodivergence are frequently
co-presented, but provider co-presentation does not establish that all members
of these audiences identify with each other or prefer that grouping.

The evidence implication is only that discovery and provider presentation use
multiple labels. It does not establish search volume or which term Australian
clients prefer.

### 6. Role and payment clarity are structural differentiators in the visible market

Provider pages mix regulated and self-regulated roles, team and solo units,
therapy and coaching, and different rebate positions. Some pages foreground
qualifications and professional membership; others foreground lived experience,
affirmation or niche focus. PolyPsychology combines counselling and coaching;
ENM Australia explicitly draws the opposite boundary by stating that its
coaching is not psychotherapy.

Exact public fees appeared in 8/16 units. The other eight cannot be coded as
more expensive or cheaper. Published units range materially in duration,
configuration, rebates and room costs, so a single market average would be
misleading. Directory filters also blur credential awareness and ENM awareness:
a primary credential can be verified without verifying ENM competence, while a
self-described poly-awareness level is not independent credential verification.

### 7. Discovery intermediaries are useful, but their counts answer different questions

Psychology Today's filtered page is the broadest visible funnel and exposes a
highly structured combination of location, role, online access, price and issue
filters. Its displayed 174 count is not a local provider census because the
first page visibly mixes local, online-only and serves-area units and many
excerpts do not name the segment. Come Back to Bed and KAP are more explicitly
allied-aware taxonomies but are self-submission systems with clear
non-endorsement limits. SAS helps with sexology role/accreditation, while SSEAA
reveals adjacent coaching/bodywork/education. Polyfriendly is global and showed
count inconsistency and duplicate records in the accessible sample.

The implication for later owner synthesis is that directory presence,
professional-role verification and explicit on-page ENM presentation are three
separate discovery signals. None can safely substitute for the others.

## Contradictions, access limits and unresolved checks

- The sample is purposive and visibility-led. It is not a census of all WA or
  Australian practitioners.
- Psychology Today's page displayed 174 results but only 20 cards on the first
  extracted page, across nine pages, and combined local, online-only and
  serves-area records. Counts may change with personalisation, availability,
  pagination or platform data.
- The Psychology Today category placed many providers whose visible excerpt did
  not mention ENM/polyamory. Those category-only cards were not promoted into
  the direct provider sample unless their sampled profile/page explicitly named
  the segment.
- Come Back to Bed's 43 is a manual count of profile headings, not a platform
  total. The sections include a US provider and a WA provider under `Online
  Only`; counts must remain directory records, not distinct local clinicians.
- KAP displayed 24 Australian listings and 3 WA listings. It combines many
  professional categories and its awareness levels are provider submissions.
- Evolve Together still displayed a 25%-off promotion ending 30 June 2026 on
  17 July 2026. The standard fee was behind an external booking interface and
  could not be reproduced, so no current price was recorded.
- Corina Johns's linked direct website and Love In Mind's legacy fee URL did not
  load in the research interface. Tania Rose's downloadable fee schedule and
  embedded booking prices for Kendall Buckley/Evolve Together were not exposed.
- Online service eligibility was coded only when explicitly stated. Several
  providers may serve more places than recorded; `not observed` is not an
  absence claim.
- Exact availability/waitlists were not systematically exposed and were not
  inferred from active booking links.
- Prices and rebate statements are volatile observations. They require
  re-checking before public comparison or commercial decision use.
- No provider quality, competence, clinical appropriateness, demand, uptake or
  audience preference is established by these pages or listings.
